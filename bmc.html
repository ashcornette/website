draft — week 2 · business model canvas · phase 1 foundations

The model

a freemium consumer app. receipts go in, a restock date comes out. two lines of revenue sit on top — a subscription that lifts the item cap, and an affiliate cut taken at the moment someone reorders. the affiliate cut stays free for everyone, because it only pays at volume.

revised where phase-2 research broke the week-1 assumptions: manual logging is out, and neither "no AI" nor "private and offline" survives as a wedge — Restock planner already claims both. the learned timing has to carry it alone.

The canvas

the accent marks the two blocks the model turns on — what we promise, and what pays for it

Cost structure
What it costs to operate
  • engineering & design salaries — the dominant line
  • parser maintenance — recurring, not a one-off build
  • app store commission — 15–30% of every subscription
  • paid acquisition — measured against affiliate margin
  • infra stays low by design — inference runs on-device
Revenue streams
How you make money
  • free — full capture, capped at 10 tracked items
  • premium — unlimited items + household sharing
  • £2.99 / month · hypothesis, untested
  • affiliate commission at reorder — free tier included
  • the cap is the meter; the affiliate needs the volume

the spine reads left to right — partners and parsers feed the model, the model produces a warning, the warning produces a reorder. the reorder is where the money is, which is why it can't sit behind a paywall.

Where the free / paid line sits
  • capture is free — the model needs the receipts more than the user needs to pay for them
  • the item cap is the meter — it bites once the app has proved useful, not before
  • affiliate links stay free — a cut of one reorder is worth less than a paywall costs in volume
  • inbox access is asked for at free signup, not at upgrade — the trust conversation happens once, and early
The catch — to stress-test in research
the whole canvas rests on one dependency: inbox access. if Gmail or Apple tighten it, the wedge is gone and we are another manual tracker. two more to probe — receipts miss anything bought in-store with cash, and a purchase interval is only a proxy for depletion: people stockpile, share, and skip. and the price is a guess. the priced waitlist has to set it before the subscription line means anything.
