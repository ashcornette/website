draft — week 3 · three fits + mvp

The three fits

Problem–solution fit

Are you solving a real and important problem?

Running out of essentials without warning is a frequent, high-intensity pain. Fixed delivery schedules don't match real usage. Manual tracking doesn't stick.

Strong — real pain, no good solution exists
Product–market fit

Do the right customers want this and keep using it?

Target: busy multi-person households. Shared view and two-tap logging are designed for retention. Key risk: will users actually log consistently?

Solid premise — validate logging behaviour first
Business model fit

Can the product make money and sustain itself?

Free tier + premium (household sharing). Affiliate links at reorder intent = high-conversion moment. Primary revenue stream still needs to be defined.

Needs work — subscription vs. affiliate?

MVP recommendation

— recommended

Concierge MVP

Manually alert 5–10 households when items run low. No app needed. Tests the riskiest assumption: do people act on alerts?

 

Piecemeal MVP

Google Sheets + push notifications simulate the product. Zero custom dev — good for a class prototype.

 

Landing page

Tests demand, not behaviour. Weakest option — demand isn't the question here, behaviour change is.

Build · measure · learn

Riskiest assumption

Users will log consumption consistently

Metric to define before building

% who log 3+ items in first 7 days

Success signal

User acts on a low-stock alert without being prompted

behaviour beats opinion. define your metric before you build.
