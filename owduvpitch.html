introduction

the average household makes six store runs a month.

at about $174 each.¹ this pitch is about the seventh — the run you didn't plan.

we're ash cornette and nico jan — two designers who spent twelve weeks treating that seventh trip like a startup problem.

¹ drive research, u.s. grocery shopper survey, april 2024 (n = 1,000).

vision & mission

a home that quietly restocks itself.

vision

mission

business model

free until it proves itself.

free — full loop, metered
the habit
the whole loop — capture, cadence, nudge, reorder links — on a capped set of items. the cap is the meter.
owduv plus · $1.99/mo or $14.99/yr
the household
unlimited items · shared household sync · multi-inbox, multi-retailer · full history. you pay for room.
two revenue lines
the subscription lifts the cap; an affiliate cut lands at the reorder. affiliate pays at volume — so reorder links never sit behind the paywall.
acquisition
app-store intent + mental-load content — and every shared household pulls in its members.
positioning

the predictive, low-effort corner is empty. we sit in it alone.

for busy households that keep running out of everyday essentials, owduv is the restocking app that warns you before you run out — not after — by learning how fast your household actually uses things.

amazon is hands-off but blind; the lists are flexible but hand-fed. learned timing is the moat — "private" and "no ai" are already claimed.

strategic plan

eighteen months, one honest metric.

this category loses everyone past month three — so retention is the metric: month-two ≥ 40%, kill under 25%.
