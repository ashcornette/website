draft — week 7 · core user flows · phase 3

user flows

two core task flows, built on the same premise as the capture loop — owduv does the watching, and the person only acts at the moment of intent. capture is documented separately; these are what happens once a prediction fires.

Reorder — nudge to done

the conversion path. owduv has already predicted the run-out, so the person's job shrinks to a glance and a confirm. a suggested quantity and a saved retailer mean the whole thing can be three taps.

the handoff deliberately leaves the app — owduv doesn't process payment or hold a cart; it hands a pre-filled order to a retailer the person already uses. the confirmed purchase then returns as a fresh signal, sharpening the next prediction rather than resetting it.

To validate
whether people trust a suggested quantity they didn't set, and whether leaving the app for checkout breaks the sense that owduv "handled it." the snooze and dismiss paths aren't drawn here — they're the next branch to map.
Household sharing — one shared shelf

the multi-person flow. one person sets up the household; everyone else joins with a link. from there the stock view is shared, and anyone's purchase updates what everyone sees — so no single person has to be the designated tracker.

joining is kept low-friction — a link or code, no heavy account step — because the coordinator's whole problem is getting other people to take part at all. once joined, the shared view stays current off the same purchase signals, so it never depends on members remembering to log.

To validate
whether shared visibility alone settles the fairness friction, or whether households still want explicit assignment ("you're up"). and how a purchase made by a member who isn't the account owner gets attributed cleanly.
